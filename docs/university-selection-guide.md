# University Selection Guide for CSC

This guide turns the `C9`, `985`, and `211` labels into a practical application strategy.

## First: These Are Tiers, Not Exact Rankings

For CSC applicants, it is safer to think of them like this:

- `C9` = the most elite subgroup
- `985` = top national research universities
- `211` = strong national universities, including all `985` schools

In other words:

- every `C9` university is also `985` and `211`
- every `985` university is also `211`
- not every `211` university is `985`

Also important:

- `985` and `211` were replaced officially by the newer `Double First-Class` policy in 2017
- people still use `C9`, `985`, and `211` all the time when discussing prestige and difficulty
- different websites may show slightly different total counts for `211` because of mergers, renamed institutions, branch campuses, and historical counting methods

## How To Think About Applications

Do not choose only by prestige.

For CSC, this order is usually smarter:

1. supervisor fit
2. department fit
3. research area fit
4. program language
5. scholarship competitiveness
6. university tier

If a professor in a `211` university matches your topic much better than a random professor in a `C9` university, the `211` option can be the better application.

## Recommended Application Mix

If you are applying broadly, use a balanced list:

- `2 to 3` `C9` universities
- `3 to 5` `985` non-`C9` universities
- `2 to 4` strong `211`-only universities

## Practical Meaning of Each Tier

### `C9`

Use these when:

- your profile is already strong
- your research proposal is clear
- you have a strong professor match
- you are comfortable with high competition

### `985` non-`C9`

This is often the best main target range for CSC applicants.

Why:

- still high prestige
- strong labs and departments
- usually better odds than the most competitive `C9` schools

### `211`-only

These are not weak universities.

Many are excellent in specific fields such as:

- telecommunications
- electronics
- transport
- finance
- law
- geoscience
- agriculture

## How To Use The Repository

Use these files together:

- `data/universities/china_university_tiers.csv`
- `data/universities/master_university_contacts.csv`
- `workbooks/master_university_contacts.xlsx`

Workflow:

1. filter `china_university_tiers.csv` by your target tier
2. copy target universities into the master contacts workbook
3. add departments and professors
4. rank professors by research fit and responsiveness
5. only then decide your final application list
